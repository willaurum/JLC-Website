Visit Monday

Weekly meetings

A relaxed hour with structure that helps.

Meetings are designed to be approachable, useful, and welcoming whether you are brand new to Japanese or already comfortable speaking.

When

Mondays, 5:30-6:30 PM

Where

DeMoss Hall 3285

Format

Conversation, practice, and activities

Meeting vibe

Relaxed, welcoming, and built for actual participation.

There is enough structure to keep the hour meaningful, but not so much pressure that it stops being enjoyable.

What a meeting looks like

Simple flow. Easy to join.

Each week can vary, but most meetings follow a familiar rhythm that makes it easier to settle in and participate.

  1. Settle in

    Members arrive, get situated, and ease into the room without needing to perform right away.

  2. Learn together

    The main topic or practice focus is introduced with enough context for everyone to follow along.

  3. Use the language

    Conversation, small-group work, or interactive activities help the material turn into something practical.

  4. Wrap and connect

    Members leave with clearer direction, better familiarity, and a stronger sense of community than when they arrived.

First Monday

You can show up exactly as you are.

Whether you are confident, rusty, shy, or completely new, there is room for you to participate in a way that feels manageable.

Helpful expectations

What helps people feel comfortable.

  • Bring a notebook and something to write with.
  • Do not worry about knowing every word that comes up.
  • Let leadership know if this is your first time visiting.
  • Focus on participating, not getting everything perfect.

Ways to participate

There is more than one good way to join in.

Bring this

A notebook and a willingness to try.

You do not need an elaborate setup. Something simple to take notes with is enough.

Do not worry about

Being at the same level as everyone else.

Meetings are built for a range of learners, and nobody is expected to be perfect.

Good starting move

Ask where you should jump in.

Leadership can help you find the pace or group that makes the first meeting feel much more comfortable.

Beyond the weekly rhythm

Special nights keep the semester lively.

Alongside regular practice, the club can make room for themed activities that keep language learning social and memorable.

Cultural spotlights

Explore traditions, holidays, media, or everyday cultural details that add depth to the language.

Conversation nights

Spend more of the hour in guided speaking practice with prompts that fit multiple levels.

Community moments

Games, themed activities, and shared experiences help the room feel more connected over time.

Plan your visit

If you have been meaning to come, use this Monday.

One visit is enough to see the tone of the room, meet leadership, and decide how you want to keep learning from there.

Quick plan

Show up at 5:30, say hello, and ease into the hour.

If you want to know what resources or community spaces are available after the meeting, ask an officer before you head out.
