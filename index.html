Your first visit

The easiest way to join is to just show up.

You do not need prior Japanese experience, perfect confidence, or a friend group already waiting for you. If you are interested, that is enough.

Feeling nervous is normal. Most members started the same way and were glad they came anyway.

First meeting checklist

What to expect when you walk in.

  • Say hello, settle in, and meet a few people at your own pace.
  • Follow the main activity and join practice when you feel ready.
  • Ask leadership what level or group feels right for you.
  • Leave with a clearer sense of what learning path fits best.

A semester with the club

Built for momentum, not burnout.

The club balances weekly habits with special moments so learning stays steady and the community keeps getting stronger.

  1. Weekly practice

    Regular meetings give you a dependable place to keep hearing, speaking, and using Japanese with other learners.

  2. Skill-building nights

    Some meetings lean into vocabulary, conversation prompts, or focused activities that help you strengthen specific skills.

  3. Culture and community

    Games, themed nights, and cultural exploration keep the room energized and make the language feel more alive.

  4. Support for next steps

    Whether you are just starting or already committed, the club helps you find practical ways to keep improving between meetings.

Join the club

Quick answers

Common first questions.

Do I need any Japanese experience?

No. Beginners are welcome, and there is room to grow from there.

What should I bring?

A notebook is enough. If you like using a laptop or tablet, that works too.

Are there any fees?

The club is meant to be accessible, and the core learning support happens through the community itself.
